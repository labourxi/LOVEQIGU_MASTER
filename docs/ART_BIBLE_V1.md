## 1. Product Temperament

LOVEQIGU AR companion should feel like:

- an Eastern visual scripture unfolding in the real world
- a contemplative field guide rather than a game
- a ritualized observation system rather than a combat system
- a collectible cultural atlas rather than a fantasy card product

The first user feeling should be:

> I am not playing a mobile game. I am opening an ancient, living atlas.

### Explicit exclusions

Do not make the product feel like:

- a game shell
- a xianxia fantasy system
- a secondary-world RPG
- a card-battle framework
- a loot-driven reward machine

The visual tone must keep the product anchored in:

- observation
- return
- presence
- inscription
- remembrance

## 2. Visual Philosophy

The visual system should answer one question:

> Why does AR belong in LOVEQIGU?

Answer:

- because AR can reveal what is already present
- because AR can make a field moment feel witnessed
- because AR can turn exploration into a visible connection, not a mechanical task

### Core visual theses

1. Eastern, not decorative
2. Ritual, not theatrical
3. High-grade, not playful
4. Calm, not noisy
5. Observational, not explosive
6. Material, not synthetic

- casino reward shine

## 3. Color System

The color system should feel like:

- mineral pigments
- lacquer and ink
- bronze and paper
- warm metallic accents

It should not feel:

- neon
- candy-bright
- futuristic blue-purple
- gambling-red dominant

### Usage rules

- use 1 dominant dark anchor and 1 warm paper ground
- keep gold as a controlled highlight, not a full-theme wash
- reserve red for sacred emphasis and completion
- avoid overusing a single hue family
- preserve contrast for legibility over ornamental density

### Background logic

- primary field: 宣纸白 or warm paper
- ritual panels: deep 玄青 or ink-washed gray
- completion state: gold + paper + small red seal
- commercial state: subdued, L1-clean, not ceremonial

## 7. Motion Principles

Motion should explain recognition, not excitement.

### Motion vocabulary

- particles
- flowing light
- spiral expansion
- star ignition
- woven drift
- seal descent
- field breathing

### Motion pacing

- start quietly
- reveal gradually
- stabilize decisively
- end with a visible resting state

### Motion hierarchy

1. the field changes first
2. then the node or seal appears
3. then the inscription or collectible resolves
4. then the system settles

### Motion restraint

- do not over-animate every state
- do not stack multiple spectacle effects
- do not let motion obscure reading
- do not use motion as a replacement for visual clarity
